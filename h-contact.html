Help
Frequently Asked Questions
How long does it take to get a reply?

We usually respond within 24–48 hours.

Why does this website use multiple weather data providers?

We use multiple weather providers to improve accuracy and reliability. Weather conditions can vary by location and time, and no single provider is always 100% accurate. By comparing data from multiple trusted sources, we reduce errors, fill data gaps, and provide more consistent weather information.

How should I trust data from multiple providers?

Different weather services use various data sources — satellites, ground stations, and forecasting models — and update at different times. By showing data from multiple trusted providers, we help cross-check and compare forecasts, giving a broader and more reliable picture. For official alerts, always refer to IMD.

Do I need an account to submit feedback?

No, you can submit feedback without any registration.

Is this a government-authorised website?

No. This website is an independent, community-focused weather observation and information platform. It is not operated, owned, or officially authorised by any government agency.

Can I report a website issue here?

Yes — select "Report an Issue" in the contact form and describe the problem in detail.

More
Additional Questions
How often is the weather information updated?

Updates depend on the data source and may occur every few minutes to a few hours. Forecast updates use the automated system. The IMD alert shown on the home page is manually updated by the developer.

Does this site show real-time weather or forecasts?

The site displays both current conditions and forecasted weather, depending on the page and data availability.

What does "feels like" temperature mean?

"Feels like" temperature represents how the weather actually feels to the human body, taking humidity, wind, and other factors into account.

Why does the forecast sometimes change during the day?

Weather forecasts change as new data becomes available from satellites, radar, and local observations.

How far ahead is the forecast reliable?

Short-term forecasts (1–3 days) are usually more reliable. Long-term forecasts are best used as general guidance only.

Why is rainfall shown as a percentage?

The percentage represents the probability of rain occurring in the area, not how long or how heavy the rain will be.

Why might my location's weather differ from nearby areas?

Local factors such as buildings, water bodies, elevation, and wind patterns can cause small variations in weather even across short distances.
